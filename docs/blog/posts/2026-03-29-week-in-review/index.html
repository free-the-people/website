Democrats flip Florida state legislative district [Link]

Emily Gregory, Florida House of Representatives

Across the country, Democrats are winning seats that have traditionally been held by Republicans since Donny 2-Dolls returned to office last year. The most recent seat flipped was a state House seat in the same Florida district that includes Trump's Mar-a-lago resort. This is the 29th seat flipped by Democrats in the past year.

In 2024, Trump won that same district by 17 points. Gregory's election is a clear sign that his support is slipping nationwide. Although Trump has lived most of his life in New York, his personal residence and voter registration are in Florida. Despite his many claims that mail-in voting is fraudulent, Donny 2-Dolls voted by mail in this election.

Idaho refuses to turn over voter data to Trump regime [Link]

Idaho state flag.

In 2024, nearly 70% of Idaho voters voted for Donny 2-Dolls to be their president. Now, the Trump regime is demanding states turn over their voter registration records to the federal government, and this Trump-supporting state is refusing to comply with this unconstitutional order.

Idaho secretary of State Phil McGrane has stated that it is his duty to safeguard the sensitive voter data, and he does not believe the Justice Department can protect that data. This is based off of the numerous documented instances of the Trump regime mishandling sensitive data, particularly from DOGE employees.

House GOP rejects Senate deal to end DHS shutdown [Link]

United States House of Representatives

Last week, the Senate unanimously agreed to a partial funding bill that would allow TSA officers, the Coast Guard, and other employees of DHS with the exception of Customs and Border Patrol. But the House rejected that bill and passed their own bill that would include full funding for DHS (including ICE) for eight weeks.

Both the House and Senate have to agree on the same version of the same bill before it can be taken to the president to sign into law.

Donny 2-Dolls has declared yet another national emergency in order to bypass the Constitutional provision granting Congress exclusive control over the country's finances, and has ordered TSA officers to receive their back pay for their work since the shutdown began 44 days ago. The Coast Guard, FEMA, and other DHS employees will not receive their pay.

The Senate and House are now in recess for the next two weeks, so there is no chance that a resolution will come until both chambers return to work sometime in April.
