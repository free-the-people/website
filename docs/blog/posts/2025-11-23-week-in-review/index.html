Trump regime's bungling could cause massive corporate losses [Link]

For years, the United States has been considering abandoning the penny because it costs more to mint that it's worth. Other countries facing similar issues have opted to make their low-value coins out of cheaper materials or eliminate them altogether. But either way, they planned for the inevitable consequences.

Not the Trump regime, though. They just decided to stop minting the penny, and provided no explanations, nor any rules or guidelines for how future transactions should be handled. Many states have laws saying credit card customers can't be charged less for a purchase than cash customers, so businesses can't just round up their transactions to the nearest nickel without facing potential lawsuits. And if they round all their transactions down, they stand to lose millions of dollars in revenue.

Then there's the banks...if they cash a check and don't have exact change, they are required to round up, not down so they don't short-change the customer. Same goes for businesses that pay employees in cash.

And on, and on, and on, all throughout our already-crumbling economy.

They just said 'Let's get rid of it' and moved on, but they've created a train wreck...

There's been no announcement as to why, This was done without any planning or forethought as to what would happen and how it would cascade through the economy.

97% of ICE detainees have no criminal record [Link]

Image by Paul Goyette from Wikimedia Commons. Licensed under the Creative Commons Attribution-Share Alike 4.0 International license.

The Trump regime has repeatedly claimed that their extreme anti-immigrant policies are laser-focused on getting the "worst of the worst" criminals out of our country. But the facts tell a different story.

Of the 600 individuals still being detained after months of brutal raids across Chicago over the past few months, only 16 (2.6%) had any criminal history at all, and none of those criminal charges included offenses that the Trump regime has previously described as the "worst of the worst" (such as rape and murder).

U.S. District Judge Jeffrey Cummings has stated that he will order the release of these detainees if the government cannot provide compelling information that they pose a credible threat to public safety.

Chicago residents are infuriated over the violent ICE raids in their communities. They have watched their neighbors, including school teachers, childcare providers, and day laborers, targeted and disappeared in front of their eyes. This includes US citizens, legal residents, and undocumented immigrants who are in the US legally awaiting court cases.
