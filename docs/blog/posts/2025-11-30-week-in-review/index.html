James Comey and Letitia James cases dropped (for now) [Link]

Image by Joseph V M from Pixabay.

A federal judge has dismissed the Trump regime's cases against Letitia James and James Comey. After Halligan's disastrous bungling of the case against Comey last week, you might think the cases were thrown out because of something she did wrong, but it wasn't how the cases were handled that was the problem. It was who was handling them.

It turns out that Lindsey Halligan's appointment to the position of US Attorney for the Eastern District of Virginia was unlawful. Halligan was appointed as an interim U.S. attorney by Pam Bondi as ordered by Donny 2-Dolls via social media post. But the Attorney General does not have the authority to make such an appointment. Only federal judges in the district in question can make an interim appointment to serve until the vacancy can be filled.

The case against Letitia James could be restarted once the US Attorney for the Eastern District of Virginia is properly appointed, but the statute of limitations has expired in the case against James Comey, meaning there can be no further indictment.

Two National Guard members shot, one killed in Washington DC [Link]

Left: U.S. Army Specialist Sarah Beckstrom, West Virginia National Guard Soldier shot on November 26. She died of her wounds the next day.
Right: U.S. Air Force Staff Sergeant Andrew Wolfe, West Virginia National Guard Airmen shot on November 26. He is currently in critical condition.

Two members of the National Guard were shot this week in Washington D.C. Both were critically wounded and taken to the hospital. One of the victims, U.S. Army Specialist Sarah Beckstrom, has died from her injuries. The other victim, U.S. Air Force Staff Sergeant Andrew Wolfe remains in critical condition at the time of this writing.

The shooter, Rahmanullah Lakanwal, was detained after being shot and injured by guardsmen. He is an Afghan national who immigrated to the United States in 2021 when the US pulled out of Afghanistan. Lakanwal is one of tens of thousands of Afghan allies who worked with the CIA and military in the fight against the Taliban who were resettled in the United States.

The exact motives of the shooting are uncertain at this time, though it is believed that the shooting was the act of one individual and not part of a larger threat. But the Trump regime is using the shooting to expand their rhetoric about the dangers posed by immigrants. Even though immigrants are statistically less likely to commit crimes than citizens, the Trump regime has used its xenophobia to blame immigrants for everything wrong with this country, and now they are calling for the expulsion of legal immigrants from at least 19 unnamed countries deemed to be "high-risk."

Since the founding of our nation, our military members have been expected to use their judgment to distinguish between lawful and unlawful orders from their superiors, and can be held accountable for any unlawful actions that they take, even under direct order. This is because we believe our soldiers are capable of understanding which orders are legal and which are not.

After World War II ended, Hitler's sycophant's were hunted down and tried for war crimes. They all tried to excuse their actions by claiming that they were "just following orders." But that was not recognized on the world stage as a valid excuse for their blatant war crimes. Since that time, our military (like many others) has expended great effort to ensure that our service members know that they have an obligation to disobey unlawful orders.

Last week, six veterans serving in Congress posted a video in which they each reminded active duty service members that they not only can, but must disobey unlawful orders.

The Trump regime's response was to call these members of Congress seditious and publicly call for their trial and execution. That's right: Donald Trump—the same man who pardoned criminals tried, convicted, and sentenced for seditious conspiracy—is now accusing sitting members of Congress of the same crime, though they did nothing other than post the following reminder of the standing laws of our nation.
