The Ugly...

Trump regime strips legal right to bond hearings from millions of immigrants [Link]

Image by Bobak Ha'Eri from Wikimedia Commons. Licensed under the Creative Commons Attribution 3.0 Unported license.

When someone is convicted of a crime, their trial often comes months or even years after the initial charges. Courts may hold a bail bond hearing to determine whether to offer a conditional release of the defendant from jail until the trial. If approved, the defendant can deposit an agreed-upon sum of money to the court as a promise that they will return for their hearing. When the defendant returns for their trial, the money is returned. If the defendant does not return for the trial, they forfeit the money. This keeps the defendant out of jail until and unless they are convicted and sentenced.

Under new guidance from the Trump regime, anyone snatched up by ICE will be kept detained "for the duration of their removal proceedings." With plenty of new funding for concentration camps approved in the recently-passed budget reconciliation bill, there will be enough space to keep all of these detainees imprisoned until they are eventually deported.

As pointed out earlier, 65% of those detained by ICE have no criminal history at all. We're not talking about violent criminals who endanger American citizens. We're talking about long-time, peaceful residents of the United States. People with lives, families, homes, and jobs. Our neighbors, our coworkers, our friends, our community members, locked up in concentration camps far from their homes with no contact with the outside world, locked up in cages for what could be years of their lives.

This is not only un-American. It is inhuman.

ICE agents harassing volunteers assisting immigrants in court [Link]

Image by Sarahmirk from Wikimedia Commons. Licensed under the Creative Commons Attribution-Share Alike 4.0 International license.

The string of abuses perpetrated by ICE agents this year alone has infuriated Americans from all walks of life. Many people have volunteered to help immigrants navigate the path to legal residency through the courts. But the idea of extending a helping hand to those in need (while observing the law) deeply disturbs the kind of person who would willingly work for Immigration and Customs Enforcement, the American Gestapo, in this day and age.

These volunteers report being obstructed and harassed by ICE agents even though they are not violating any laws or interfering with any ICE operations. Apparently, just the thought that a person could actually care about someone who was born in another country is enough to trigger hostility in the agents. These masked agents, too cowardly to show their faces, will gang up in groups of a dozen or more to try to intimidate three or fewer volunteers, often retired women, trying to escort a scared immigrant to their legal hearing.

Volunteers attend court hearings and collect contact information from the immigrants prior to their hearings so they know who to contact if the immigrant is taken into custody. Volunteers sometimes escort the immigrants to and from the courtroom, leading them past armed and masked agents lining the courthouse hallways.

Stop and think about this...these are immigrants attending court hearings...that means they are actively obeying the laws of our country and having their cases ruled on by judges. The outcome of the hearing should be what determines how they are treated and whether they get to stay in the country or not. But instead, we have lazy immigration agents hanging out in our court houses and plucking up the immigrants who are trying to obey the laws of our country while they harass anyone who shows the slightest bit of human compassion. It requires a very special kind of evil for these agents to carry out such shameful behavior...and an equal evil for anyone, anywhere, to support such outrageous actions on the part of government agents of any kind.
